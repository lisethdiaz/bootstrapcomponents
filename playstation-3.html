PlayStation 3

The PlayStation 3 development would be announced in 2005. It was first released on November 11, 2006 in Japan and would be released worldwide by the spring of 2007. It was developed by Sony Computer Entertainment Inc. The console on this PlayStation had a complex build and architecture. It had further features by including new Blu-ray Disk technology as storage and a purpose-built Cell processor. It was also the first PlayStation model to have the ability to united with other social gaming services and be made with HD. This model supported HDMI outputs and a 108-p resolution. Hardware from the previous model, The PlayStation 2, was integrated into the new console. The controller for the PlayStation 3 was the old-style DualShock build. This PlayStation did not release exclusive games along with the console, which many people seemed to have an opinion about. The price would be $499 for the standard model and $599 for the 60GB model. Due to a drastic price change, the PlayStation 3 did not have great sales the year of release. Nonetheless, with time the PlayStation 3 would be a more affordable option. It would ultimately outsell the Xbox 360. Eventually Sony would release two models of the PlayStation 3, The Slim model released in 2009 and The Super Slim model released in 2012. These both were thinner and lighter versions with software modifications. All countries but Japan would discontinue production and shipments in 2016. In 2017 Japan would announce production of the PlayStation 3 was discontinued.
